Dynamic vs Static Stretches

Stretches
Dynamic stretching is used to stretch muscles while the body is moving.

  1. It activates muscles you will use during your workout.

  2. Dynamic stretching improves range of motion.

  3. Dynamic stretches improve body awareness.

  4. Warming up in motion enhances muscular performance and power.
Static stretching is used to stretch muscles while the body is at rest.

  1. Holding the position for 30–60 seconds will increase flexibility in the tissue.

  2. Done prior to activity, static stretching may actually inhibit the muscle’s ability to fire.

  3. Best if done after the activity.

  4. Improves your muscle interactions and should be done to maintain range of motion
    and keep the muscle tissue healthy.
Proper Excercise Routine
  1. Get your heart to pump. Go for a jog or ride a bike on low resistence.

  2. Do some dynamic stretches.

  3. Hit your workout. Going to play a sport, or just workout at the gym?

  4. Do some static Stretches
